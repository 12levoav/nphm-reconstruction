Identity, Expression, and Head Pose Reconstruction

Exploring the performance of deep learning models on identity, expression, and head pose estimation tasks.

Overview

This project explores the use of various deep learning models for identity, expression, and head pose reconstruction. Through experiments on models like EMOCA, ResNet, and 6DRepNet, we evaluate their performance under different configurations such as the use of augmentation, MSE loss, L1 loss, and Geodesic Distance loss.

Best Model Results

  • Identity Reconstruction: EMOCA with MSE and augmentation (Cosine Similarity: 0.1422)
  • Expression Reconstruction: EMOCA with MSE and augmentation (Cosine Similarity: 0.27)
  • Head Pose Estimation: 6DRepNet with Geodesic Distance loss and no augmentation (MAEV: 0.83)

Challenges

Identity reconstruction was hindered by the limited number of unique participants in the dataset, while head pose estimation faced challenges due to extreme head orientations and dataset imbalances. Future research should focus on better handling of these issues to improve model performance.
